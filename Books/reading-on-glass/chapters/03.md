# Publishing Your Own Book

Publishing to this store takes about five minutes and no special tools.

## Step by step

1. Create a folder under `Books` named after your book: lowercase letters, numbers, and hyphens. For example, `Books/sea-stories`.
2. Inside it, create `book.json`. Copy one from an existing book and edit the fields — remember that `id` must match your folder name.
3. Create a `chapters` folder and write your chapters as Markdown files. Number them (`01.md`, `02.md`, …) so they sort sensibly in the repository, and list them in the manifest in reading order.
4. Commit and push. If the project is connected to Vercel, the push triggers a deployment automatically, and your book is on the shelf when it finishes — usually within a minute or two.

## Checking your work

Before pushing, you can preview locally by running the development server and opening the site in your browser. Your book should appear on the shelf with its generated cover. Open it and skim each chapter: the most common mistakes are a manifest that lists a chapter file that does not exist, or a JSON syntax error — a missing comma is the classic.

If a book does not appear on the shelf at all, its `book.json` almost certainly failed to parse. The build logs will say so.

## A note on what belongs here

Everything in this store ships as readable text in a public repository, so publish only what you have the right to publish: your own writing, or work in the public domain. The classics are a deep well — Aesop is already on the shelf, and he has plenty of company waiting.

Happy writing, and happier reading.
