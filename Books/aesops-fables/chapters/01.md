# Wolves and Shepherds

## The Boy Who Cried Wolf

A shepherd boy tended his flock on a hillside near a village. The days were long and dull, and one afternoon, for the fun of it, he ran toward the village shouting, "Wolf! Wolf! A wolf is at the sheep!"

The villagers dropped their work and came running with sticks and staves — and found the boy laughing at them, the sheep grazing peacefully behind him.

A few days later he played the trick again, and again the villagers came, and again he laughed.

Then one evening a wolf truly came down from the hills and fell upon the flock. The boy ran toward the village screaming, "Wolf! Wolf!" — but the villagers only shook their heads at one another and stayed where they were. No one believed him, and the wolf scattered and slaughtered the flock at its leisure.

> A liar will not be believed, even when he speaks the truth.

## The Wolf in Sheep's Clothing

A wolf found it hard to get at a certain flock, for the shepherd watched his sheep closely. One day the wolf came upon a sheepskin that had been cast aside, and an idea took shape. He draped the skin over his back and slipped in among the flock at pasture, and the shepherd counted him with the rest.

That evening the wolf was shut into the fold with the sheep, and he thought his fortune was made. But in the night the shepherd, wanting mutton for his table, went into the fold in the dark, took hold of the first animal his hand fell upon — and killed the wolf on the spot.

> Those who pretend to be what they are not may be undone by their own disguise.

## The Shepherd and the Wolf Cub

A shepherd found a wolf cub abandoned in the hills. He carried it home, raised it among his dogs, and taught it to guard the flock. The cub grew into a strong wolf and seemed as faithful as any hound.

But whenever a wild wolf stole a sheep and the dogs gave chase, the tame wolf ran with them — and when the dogs turned back, he would go on, share in the feast, and trot home innocent. And when no wolves came raiding for a while, he took to killing sheep quietly himself.

At last the shepherd caught him at it, and hanged him from the nearest tree.

> What is bred in the bone will come out in the flesh.
