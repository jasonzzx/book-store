# Tortoises and Hares

## The Tortoise and the Hare

A hare was forever boasting of his speed. "I have never been beaten," he said, "and I never shall be. I challenge anyone here to race me."

"I accept," said the tortoise quietly.

The hare laughed until he fell over. But the race was set, the course was marked, and off they went. The hare shot out of sight almost at once — so far ahead that, to show his contempt, he lay down beside the road for a nap.

The tortoise plodded on. And on. And on. When the hare woke, the sun was low, and far up the road he saw the tortoise a step from the finish line. He ran as he had never run, but he was too late by exactly one step.

"Well," said the tortoise, catching his breath. "Slow and steady wins the race."

> Slow and steady wins the race.

## The Eagle and the Tortoise

A tortoise, discontented with her lowly life, watched the birds and sighed. She begged an eagle to teach her to fly, offering all the treasures of the pond in payment.

"You were not made for the air," said the eagle. But the tortoise pleaded and pleaded, until at last the eagle took her up in his talons, carried her high above the fields — and let go.

Flying, the tortoise discovered, was easy. It was the landing that undid her.

> Those who insist against their nature must bear the consequences.

## The Ant and the Grasshopper

All through the warm summer a grasshopper sang in the grass, while a line of ants trudged past him hauling grain to their nest. "Come and sing with me," said the grasshopper. "Why toil on such a beautiful day?"

"We are storing food for the winter," said the ants, "and you would be wise to do the same." The grasshopper laughed and went on singing.

When winter came, the grasshopper, starving, dragged himself to the ants' door and begged for a share of their grain. "You sang all summer," said the ants. "Now dance all winter."

> There is a time for work and a time for play — and the one pays for the other.
